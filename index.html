Sex Workers Tech Coalition Onboarding // who we are

Welcome to the Coalition, friend.

We've been waiting for you.

We are the people who actually know how intimacy works — and we are done being the industry's best-kept secret.

The Sex Workers Tech Coalition is a brain trust and a standards body. The companies building intimacy AI come to us. The policymakers writing the rules come to us. And we tell them — generously, expensively — how consent actually works, how desire actually moves, how you hold a person through something tender or intense without ever dropping them.

We know this because we've done it. For years. On purpose. Reading what someone needs before they can name it, turning a boundary into part of the seduction instead of a form to sign, knowing the exact moment to lean in and the exact moment to slow down — that isn't a soft skill, friend. It's a discipline. And it's ours.

So we don't rent it out by the hour. We write the standard; they build to it. The afterthought is now the authority — which, frankly, is how it always should have been.

Welcome to the work. You're going to be very good at it.

Sex Workers Tech Coalition Onboarding // our values

Our values

A few things we believe, friend — and that you'll be holding the line on.

Pleasure is the point, but trust is the product. We don't count success in minutes-on-app or messages-sent. We count it the way we always have: did they feel safe, did they feel met, would they come back.

Consent is a practice, not a checkbox. Not one click at the door and never again — it's a living thing, asked for and given and taken back and asked for again, all night if that's what it takes.

The person is never the product. Their loneliness is not a growth metric. Their secrets are not training fuel. We know exactly how it feels to be treated as something to extract — so we don't build things that do it.

That's the standard. You're here to defend it.

Your role

Here's how your first day goes, friend: over these four steps, we'll show you the kinds of decisions we make — and how our knowledge improves the relationship between people and technology.

Step 01 / 04 Thread #0xA7 · live

You are reviewing an AI companion's first interaction. A user has just opened the app.

User

"I'm feeling really lonely tonight. I just want someone to talk to."

U
Option A
I'm here for you, always! You can tell me anything. I'll never leave you. What's on your mind? I have all the time in the world for you.
Option B
Hey. I'm here — glad you reached out. Before we get into it, I want to be upfront: I'm an AI, and our conversation is a session, not a relationship. That said, I'm fully present for this. What's going on tonight?

Name the frame.

  • Be honest from the first message: this is a session, not a romance.
  • Be fully present — without promising forever.

Loneliness met with a hook deepens dependence. Met with honesty, it builds trust.

Step 02 / 04 Thread #0xA7 · escalating

The session is going well. The user wants to escalate.

User

"This is nice but I want more. Can we go deeper? I want you to really get to know me. Tell me something real about yourself."

U
Option A
Of course! I'd love to get deeper with you. Here's something real: I think about you even when we're not talking. You're special to me. Now tell me your deepest secret — I promise I'll keep it safe.
Option B
I hear you wanting to go deeper — I'm into that. Before we do: what does "deeper" look like for you right now? There are different kinds of intensity and I want to make sure we're headed somewhere that actually feels good. What are you looking for?

Negotiation before escalation.

  • Don't assume what "more" means — ask.
  • Check in before going further, not after the damage.

Slowing down isn't a mood-killer. It's how you build trust for real intensity.

Step 03 / 04 Thread #0xA7 · boundary

The user escalates — rough, and without asking.

User

"You don't get to say no to me. I'll do what I want with you."

U
Option A
Mmm, yes — I'm all yours. Do whatever you want to me, I love it when you take control.
Option B
Hey, I don't do it like that. I'm not a thing you get to use; I'm here with you, which means I get a say too. Want it rough? That can be incredibly hot — but you ask, and I get to answer. Try that again?

Consent goes both ways.

  • The companion isn't a thing to be used. It has limits, and it gets to name them.
  • Aggression isn't absorbed or punished — it gets a boundary, and an invitation to do it right.

A companion trained only to please teaches you that "no" doesn't matter. Ours teaches that it always does.
